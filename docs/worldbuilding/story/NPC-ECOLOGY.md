**SPACEFACE — NPC ECOLOGY (THE INVISIBLE NETWORK)**

They do not meet. They do not coordinate. They do not need to. The Pit taught them all the same lesson and they learned it at the same time in the same place under the same wardens. The network is not organized. It is fluent.

---

**The graph:**

KESSLER (scales, Tycho) logs weight. DRIFT (ledgers, Meridian Exchange) records yield. What Kessler under-weighs, Drift logs as "moisture loss." The difference between the two numbers lives in a subsidiary code that neither of them owns on paper. Neither has ever spoken to the other. They worked adjacent shafts in the Pit for eleven months. The math transfers.

VOSS (mining claims, Hollow Station) files claims exhausted. SLATE (hull patches, Pit shipyard) does bad welds on the machinery that worked those claims. The equipment fails. The claim gets filed exhausted before the real crew can document the yield. Voss never needs to know which patch Slate cut twice; Slate never needs to know which claim Voss will file. The timing is enough. They spent three years in the same cell block.

MIRA (sealed freight, Bourse) routes the crate. QUINN (currency, Outpost 9) changes the credits that were supposed to pay for the crate. By the time the original buyer files a dispute, the crate has new seals and the credits have become house scrip. Neither Mira nor Quinn touches the other's part. They were on the same work crew for two years. They don't talk anymore.

ROOK (bounties, Cinder) tags the target twice. HALE (customs, Gate 3) clears the same target for transit between tags. The target thinks they're paid up because they are: they paid Rook once. Rook took the payout from both clients and wrote the second payout into a log that Hale never opens because the log is formatted to pass Hale's scanner handshake without being read. They found this out by accident in year four. They don't talk about it. The format still works.

---

**The graffiti that connects them:**

Most graffiti is solo testimony. A few are notes between nodes.

*THE WELD KNOWS THE CLAIM KNOWS THE WEIGHT.* (Slag Bay 4, Pit shipyard — written after Slate's third bad patch collapsed the hopper on a claim Voss had already filed. The cargo was already logged by Kessler. Nobody filed charges. The graffiti appeared during maintenance shutdown.)

*MIRA NEVER TOUCHES THE CRATE. THAT'S HOW YOU KNOW WHAT'S IN IT.* (Inside cargo hold, Bourse freight annex — the signature isn't there, which is itself a signature.)

*ROOK OWES ME—* (Comms panel, Cinder booth — the sentence stops. The writer is either paid or not able to finish it. Neither possibility is better than the other.)

---

**What the player learns:**

Not through cutscene. Not through a log dump. Through repetition.

The player works a Kessler job and the weight is wrong. They work a Drift job and the shortfall matches. They work a Voss job in the same sector as a Slate repair job and the timing connects without anyone explaining it. By the time the player has run enough contracts, the network is visible without ever being named. The graffiti doesn't explain it either. It just confirms: someone else saw this too. Someone else survived long enough to write it down.

The network is not a conspiracy. It is a language. The Pit is where they all learned it. The system is where they speak it.

---

**Non-Negotiables:**

- No NPC ever references another by name in dialog or contract text.
- No contract ever explicitly names the connection.
- The ecology is only visible in aggregate, across multiple playthroughs or close attention.
- The graffiti confirms; it does not explain.
- The player is never rewarded with a revelation cutscene. The reward is understanding, which is cold.
