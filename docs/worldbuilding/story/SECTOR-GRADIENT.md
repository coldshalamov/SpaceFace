**SPACEFACE — SECTOR GRADIENT (WHAT THE JOURNEY FEELS LIKE)**

The Conrad structure. The player starts at The Pit (S0) — already marked, already the labor colony — and the first major mission hub is Helios Prime (S1), the clean core. The journey is not innocent-to-corrupt. It is already-marked moving outward to face the mechanism that marked them. Marlow wasn't innocent when he set out. Neither is this player.

This document is not about what each sector contains mechanically. It's about what the air smells like. S0 (The Pit) is covered in vibe-04-the-pit.md.

---

**S1 (HELIOS PRIME)**

Full spectrum. 22 degrees. Food that tastes like food. The recyclers are serviced on schedule because the people who fund the recyclers live here and breathe the air. The graffiti exists but gets painted over within 48 hours — maintenance runs on a strict cycle in the inner sectors. What stays up is licensed: faction insignia, navigation markers, corporate branding. Unauthorized text is cleaned before the reader can copy it down.

The corruption here is invisible because the surfaces are maintained. This is Le Carré's London. The men in clean stations sign the orders. The paperwork is filed correctly. Nothing looks wrong. Nothing is wrong, in the sense that every law on the books has been followed. The laws were written by the people who benefit from them being followed this way.

Helios Prime is where Vale's division has its offices. The organizational chart doesn't list Atmospheric Logistics Allocation. It lists Logistics Oversight. The door looks like every other door.

---

**S2–S3 (TYCHO RELAY / MERIDIAN EXCHANGE)**

The maintenance cycle runs every 96 hours instead of 48. The graffiti gets through a full cycle before it's covered. The air quality is adequate. The temperature is 18 degrees, which is fine. The fluorescent elements have started shifting toward yellow-white — not full spectrum, but close.

The factions are visible and competing here. MTS and Concord both operate openly. The contracts board has more entries than you can take in a week. The Kessler job is here. The Drift job is here. The weight variance has been "under review" for 22 cycles.

This is where the player first notices that "under review" doesn't mean anything is going to be reviewed.

---

**S4–S5 (HOLLOW STATION / BOURSE / CONTESTED SECTORS)**

12–15 degrees. 70% spectrum. The maintenance schedule has slipped to every ten days, which means the graffiti stays up long enough to accumulate. There are layers here — new text over old text, some of it contradicting itself. The oldest text is about the Pit. People who came from the Pit settled this band of sectors first, before the money followed. Their names are on some of the older graffiti lines.

The air is within tolerance. The commissary food is pre-packaged. The factions overlap: Concord and Reach both claim jurisdiction at Gate 5. Filing a claim here requires a patience the system isn't designed to reward. Voss works Hollow. The Quiet routes through Bourse. Everything is a half-measure that somehow keeps working, the way a bad weld holds until the first pressure spike.

---

**S6–S7 (CINDER / SKERRIS DEEP / OUTER INDUSTRIAL)**

The recyclers work but they run on overextended schedules. The stations supplement with pressurized canisters — a secondary market in breathable air. The Quiet sells it alongside everything else. Nobody asks where the canisters come from. Nobody wants to know.

10–11 degrees. 55% spectrum. The lighting at this level is functional but makes everything look slightly wrong — faces, cargo manifests, wound colors. The graffiti here uses a different vocabulary than the core sectors. Longer sentences. More names. More dates. People this far out write like they expect to be understood by people who will come later, not people who are here now.

Rook is at Cinder. The bounty board is the only legitimate market in the sector. What "legitimate" means at this distance is a question the inner sectors stopped asking because the answer was too useful.

---

**S8 (VEIL EXPANSE — VAEL SPACE)**

The Vael have better air than anywhere in the outer sectors. This is not a courtesy. When the allocation queue stopped serving this region, the Vael built their own atmospheric processing network. They run it on their own maintenance schedule. The specifications for their recyclers are in a format that Concord's diagnostic systems can't read. This is not an accident.

Warm air. Good lighting. Food that requires context to appreciate — different flavoring conventions, different preparation, different temperature conventions. The Vael hospitality is real and the Vael contracts are twelve pages long and nobody in the inner sectors has read page nine.

The dissonance is important: the faction the HUD marks as "OUTSIDER — CAUTION" has the best air in the outer reaches. The faction that runs the atmospheric allocation queue doesn't service this region. The Vael didn't need them to. That's the leverage the addendum is built on.

---

**S9 (ASHFALL REACH)**

Reserve atmosphere only. The Kurtz figure's station runs on sealed tanks and a patched recycler that took eleven years to get right. The air is cold and thin and smells like hydraulic fluid over something organic that the undersized scrubbers can't clear.

The player has smelled this before. It's the lower decks of the Pit.

The recognition is supposed to land wrong. This is the end of the Conrad journey — the furthest point from the lit core — and it smells like home. Like the place they left. The darkness at the end of the river isn't alien. It's familiar. This is what Conrad's Marlow felt arriving at Kurtz's station: not horror at the unknown, but recognition of the already-known in a new context.

14 degrees. The temperature of being alive in the Pit.

The graffiti at Ashfall Reach is sparse. Most of what's here is old — from the original surveying crews, from the people who came through before it was abandoned. The Kurtz figure hasn't added any. They don't write on walls. They write in the ledger.

---

**The gradient is the game.**

The player doesn't travel to a different world when they leave Helios Prime. They travel to the truth of the world they started in. The inner sectors look the way they do because they have the resources to maintain the look. The outer sectors look the way they do because they don't. The same system, seen from increasingly honest angles.

By the time the player reaches Ashfall Reach and breathes Pit air, they have been moving through one long continuous atmosphere. The recycler allocation queue made it look like different places. It was always the same place.

The Pit is everywhere. It just has different paint.
