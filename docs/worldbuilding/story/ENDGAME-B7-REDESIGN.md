**SPACEFACE — ENDGAME REDESIGN (B7: THE DEEP REACH)**

The current spec gives the player the title "Sector Baron." This is wrong for the world.

A player who has been through the HUD's three phases of complicity, who has seen the NPC ecology operating beneath the surface, who has read enough graffiti to understand what it means — that player does not want a title. A title is what the system gives to the people the system approves of. This player knows what the system is.

The endgame is not a reward. It is a recognition. Three choices, each of which is honest about what winning actually costs.

---

**THE CHOICE (presented after reaching 100,000 credits net worth and rep ≥50 with chosen faction):**

No cutscene. No speech. A contract board update, a comms message, and a graffiti line that appears on the player's bulkhead simultaneously. The player has three contracts available. Only one can be accepted. The others expire the moment the player makes a choice.

---

**CHOICE A: THE CLEAN UNIFORM**

Contract Board: "CONCORD AUXILIARY COMMISSION — SECTOR ADMINISTRATOR APPOINTMENT"
HUD on accept: "Appointment confirmed. Record expunged. Welcome to the service."
Graffiti on bulkhead: "They let you in. That means they need something from you."

What happens: The player's faction rep with Concord goes to +700 (Hero). Their criminal record is cleared. Station surcharges disappear. Missions unlock that only Concord Auxiliary can take. The HUD stabilizes — tags return to clear, accurate IFF readings. Everything looks clean.

What the game doesn't say out loud: The first mission offered under the new appointment is a "ROUTINE CUSTOMS OPERATION." The cargo seized is listed as "CONTRABAND — 18 UNITS." The previous owner of that cargo is listed in a secondary log the player can choose to open: it's someone they worked with. The HUD logged the seizure as "LAWFUL INTERDICTION." The manifest shows the cargo already assigned to a private buyer.

The graffiti in the next airlock the player enters: *The signature is always the same. Only the paper changes.*

Endgame loop: The player is now Concord. They run the same jobs they used to run against, from the inside. The job descriptions are different. The math is the same.

---

**CHOICE B: THE SAME SILENCE**

Contract Board: "QUIET SYNDICATE — SENIOR ROUTING POSITION (UNDISCLOSED LOCATION)"
HUD on accept: "Position confirmed. Traffic begins immediately."
Graffiti on bulkhead: "You're not a person anymore. You're a channel. That's fine. Channels last longer."

What happens: The player's identity disappears from public faction records. Their name stops appearing on contract boards. A new set of mission types unlocks — "ROUTING OVERSIGHT" jobs that involve no direct action, only logistics. The player sets up the runs and watches other pilots execute them. The cut arrives automatically.

What the game doesn't say out loud: The pilots executing the routes are running contracts that look exactly like the ones the player used to run. The graffiti from those routes will appear in the same airlocks. The player's callsign will eventually show up in someone else's graffiti: "THEY NEVER SHOWED BUT THE CARGO MOVED."

Endgame loop: The player has become The Quiet. They profit from every war, every shortage, every transit that goes sideways. They are never on the kill feed. The HUD doesn't track them anymore because there's nothing to track. This is not a loss. This is the Pit with better insulation.

---

**CHOICE C: THE ONLY HONEST OPTION**

Contract Board: none. This option is a graffiti line that appears on the player's bulkhead after B6 is complete, not a board entry. It is not clickable. It is just there.

Graffiti: *THEY KNEW THE MASS. THEY ALWAYS KNEW THE MASS.*

If the player flies to the Ashfall Reach with their full load and no active contracts and initiates a jump drive charge toward the wormhole without a destination registered, the game offers a single prompt: "JUMP WITHOUT DESTINATION?" Yes / No.

If yes: the reactor overloads at the wormhole threshold. The HUD prints one last line — "CARGO: STABLE" — which is the same line it printed at the end of the first run. Then black. The kill feed appears for one second: "+1 UNKNOWN."

No score screen. No title. The campaign ends.

What the game says: nothing. What the HUD says: CARGO: STABLE. What the graffiti says: the same thing it's been saying since the first run.

The player's name appears in the graffiti at the next docking cycle, after the save resets to sandbox: "THEY KNEW WHAT THEY WERE CARRYING. THAT'S THE BEST THING YOU CAN SAY ABOUT THEM."

---

**Why these three choices and not one:**

Le Carré endings are not about winning or losing. They're about what kind of person you become in the process of surviving. George Smiley at the end of *Tinker Tailor* doesn't win. He understands. The winning is hollow. The understanding is what costs him everything.

Choice A is Smiley becoming Karla's mirror: the player becomes the institution they hated. They are now the gray men in clean stations who sign the orders.

Choice B is Smiley retiring to something that isn't retirement: the player becomes the system's lubricant. They make the rot run smoother. That's not nothing.

Choice C is the book closing itself: the player stops before they become either. Whether this is dignity or waste depends on how many contracts they ran before choosing it.

None of these choices is rewarded with a title. None of them is presented as the "good" ending. The game ends the same way it began: with the HUD showing cargo weight that doesn't match what's actually in the hold, and graffiti that knows something the system has already decided not to record.

---

**The Kurtz figure (Ashfall Reach):**

Before the endgame choice, in Ashfall Reach, the player finds a derelict station with one living occupant: a former Concord administrator who has been here for eleven years. They are not hostile. They are not raving. They have a desk, a working comms unit they use to talk to no one, and a wall covered in manifests from eleven years of cargo traffic they monitored after going off-grid.

Their name is in the Concord database as "DECEASED — SECTOR INCIDENT."

What they have: the complete ledger of every cargo transaction that was falsified in Sectors 1–6 over the past twenty years. Every Kessler weight discrepancy. Every Mira reroute. Every Hale clearance. Compiled in handwritten columns on paper, cross-referenced, with names attached.

What they say when the player arrives: "I know what you're carrying. I knew before you got here. The mass is always the same. Only the manifest changes."

They don't want anything. They're not asking to be rescued. The information is there if the player wants it. There's no mission attached to taking it. If the player takes the ledger, nothing immediately changes. It just becomes a cargo item: "PERSONAL EFFECTS — 1 UNIT." Mass: 0.4t.

The ledger is not usable as a weapon against the factions. It's not leverage. It's a record. The game has nowhere to put it. Neither does the player. That's the point.

The Kurtz figure's final line, after the player takes the ledger or declines: "The count never ends. You know that. That's why you're here."

The player's ship departs. The station doesn't flag them on leaving. The derelict returns to being a blip on the sector scan. The mass of the ledger is 0.4t and it never changes, even if the player jettisons it. The manifest still shows it.

---

**Implementation note:**

The Kurtz figure's station should be discoverable mid-game (B5 or later) via a recon_scan mission that leads somewhere the briefing said would be empty. The administrator appears in the mission log as "SCAVENGER — UNKNOWN." If the player doesn't interact with them, they leave the sector. The administrator is still there next visit. And the next. They don't go anywhere. They've been here for eleven years. A few more cycles isn't going to change anything.
