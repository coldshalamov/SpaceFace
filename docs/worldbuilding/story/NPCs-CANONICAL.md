**SPACEFACE — CANONICAL NPCs (RECURRING CONTRACT ACTORS)**

Eight names appear on contract boards and after-action logs across sectors. They do not introduce themselves. They do not explain. They transact, log, and leave. The graffiti that follows is written in the same hand that signed the manifest.

**KESSLER** logs cargo at Tycho Relay. "Weight matches prior haul." Thumb twice on the seal. Manifest logged before scan clears. Later: "variance adjustment." Graffiti (first visit): THE SCALE LIES WHEN THE THUMB IS ON IT. Graffiti (second visit, different hand): KESSLER. SCALE 4. TYCHO. TWENTY-TWO MONTHS. LOOK IT UP.

**VOSS** runs the claim recorder at Hollow Station. "Vein looks played out. Move on." Mask down. Cutter finishes. Claim filed exhausted same shift new crew arrives. Prior markers stripped. Graffiti (on the claim post): THE ROCK REMEMBERS WHO FILED FIRST. Graffiti (lower, added after, unattributed): THE SECOND SHIFT FOUND MARKERS. THEY'RE GONE NOW. VOSS WAS ON THAT SHIFT.

**MIRA** handles sealed freight at Bourse. "Route's clear. Payment on delivery." She swaps the manifest codes at the relay while the comms window stays open on the prior client's channel. The crate arrives with new seals. The original client receives the seizure notice. Graffiti inside the cargo hold: THE SEAL WAS NEVER YOURS. Graffiti at the loading dock (added later, different hand): MIRA NEVER TOUCHES THE CRATE. THAT'S HOW YOU KNOW WHAT'S IN IT.

**ROOK** coordinates bounty tags from a booth at Cinder. "Target's last ping was here. Standard fee." The stylus hovers over the payout line, then adds a second signature below it. The same target ID appears on a second board the next cycle. Rook's cut is already deducted from both. Graffiti on the comms panel: ONE NAME. TWO PAYERS. Below it, cut off: ROOK OWES ME—

**SLATE** welds hull patches at the Pit shipyard. "This'll hold till the next gate." The torch cuts the same seam twice, the second pass narrower. The patch fails at the first pressure spike. Slate is already on the next berth quoting replacement cost. Graffiti on the repair bay door: THE WELD KNOWS WHO CUT IT TWICE.

**DRIFT** records ore deliveries at Meridian Exchange. "Quota met. Credit transferred." The ledger page turns before the scale settles. The shortfall is logged as "moisture loss." Drift's own account shows the difference deposited under a subsidiary code. Graffiti above the scale: THE VEIN PAYS THE MAN WHO WEIGHS IT.

**HALE** inspects for Concord customs at Gate 3. "Scan complete. No flags." The handheld unit records the crate contents before the lid opens. The fine arrives at the next gate under a different regulation number. Hale initials both entries. Graffiti on the scanner housing: THE LOG REMEMBERS WHAT THE EYE SKIPPED. Scratched into the housing below it, smaller: HALE INITIALED BOTH. GATE 3. YEAR 7. THE SECOND ENTRY IS FILED UNDER REG 44-C. GO LOOK.

Hale is not corrupt. This is what Graham Greene would understand and what most people don't. Hale runs the scanner. The scanner says what it says. The regulation says what the regulation says. The double fine is not an action Hale takes — it is a provision of REG 44-C that Hale correctly applies. Someone else wrote the regulation. Someone else put the provision in. Hale initials the second entry because the second entry is legally required and Hale has never, in seventeen years, failed to initial a legally required entry. The priest does not look in the hold. The priest administers the sacrament. The hold is someone else's department.

**QUINN** changes credits at the bar on Outpost 9. "Rate's posted. No questions." The stack is counted once under the bar light, then again under the UV. The short count is replaced with house scrip before the customer looks down. Quinn wipes the counter with the same rag. Graffiti on the till drawer: THE COUNT ENDS WHEN THE DRAWER CLOSES. On the underside of the bar, visible only if you're sitting: QUINN KEEPS THE CHANGE.

Eight names. Contract boards. After-action logs. They transact. They log. They leave. Graffiti appears one cycle after. Station maintenance covers it. New lines appear same locations next visit.
