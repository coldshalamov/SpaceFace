**SPACEFACE — THE VETHARI (WHAT CAME FROM OUTSIDE)**

The galaxy's worst-kept secret and best-maintained lie.

---

**What the galaxy believes:**

Fermi's paradox is settled. Humanity spread across hundreds of systems over thousands of years and never found anyone else home. The absence of evidence became evidence of absence became consensus became the kind of truth that doesn't require defending because nobody's left to attack it. Aliens don't exist. The question is closed. The bars are full of people who'll tell you about the things they saw on deep-range hauls, and the bars are also full of people who know that deep-range haul stories are what happens when the recycler air gets thin and the mind fills in the gaps.

The superstitions persist. Spacers talk about ghost ships, dead-channel transmissions, cargo that was there at pickup and gone at delivery with the seals intact. The Ascendant Choir builds shrines around relics that their doctrine says predate human settlement. The Vael contract addendum — page 9, the one nobody reads — contains a clause about "non-standard biological contamination" that references a quarantine protocol for organisms not in the standard database. The clause has been in the addendum since the Vael started writing addenda. Nobody asks why.

The people who know the truth are the people who are paid to manage the truth's filing status. The truth is filed under REF 44-C in a Concord evidence processing system that has been moving sealed containers between facilities for fourteen years. The containers hold fragments of hull material that doesn't match any known manufacturing process. The fragments contain traces of biological material from a species that doesn't appear in any database. The filing status is "UNRESOLVED." It has been unresolved for fourteen years. It will remain unresolved because resolving it would require an administrative action that nobody with the authority to take it has any incentive to take.

This is not a cover-up. A cover-up implies active suppression. This is bureaucratic inertia applied to a problem that the bureaucracy was never designed to solve. The system handles customs fraud. It handles atmospheric allocation. It handles evidence storage. It handles non-human biological material the same way it handles everything else: file it, move it, initial the transfer log, don't open the container.

---

**What actually happened:**

Approximately two hundred years ago, a ship arrived in a system at the edge of human-settled space. The system contained three inhabited worlds — agricultural colonies, minor, not strategically significant. The ship was large enough to register on orbital sensors as an anomalous mass event. It did not transmit. It did not respond to hails. It entered orbit around the most populated of the three worlds and opened.

What came out of it killed approximately sixty percent of the planet's population over a period of nineteen days. The survivors who made it off-world described the attackers inconsistently — tall, armored, moving in patterns that suggested coordination but not communication, making sounds that were not language but were not random either. The descriptions were filed as trauma testimony and weighted accordingly, which is to say they were not weighted at all.

The ship moved to the second world. The same thing happened. It took twelve days because the second world had fewer people. The ship moved to the third world. Fourteen days. Then the ship left the system.

No Concord fleet responded. No military action was taken. The system was at the edge of settled space. Communications delay was significant. By the time reports reached the nearest Concord administrative center, the ship was gone. The reports were filed as "ANOMALOUS INCIDENT — FRONTIER SYSTEM — CAUSE UNDETERMINED." The three worlds were reclassified from "INHABITED — AGRICULTURAL" to "ABANDONED — ENVIRONMENTAL HAZARD." The reclassification was processed under REF 44-C because REF 44-C handles sector viability assessments and the viability score of the three worlds had dropped below the threshold. The same code. The same form. The same signature authority.

The survivors scattered. Most ended up in systems where nobody had heard of their home worlds, telling stories nobody believed, carrying memories that sounded like the recycler-air stories every spacer tells after too long in the dark. Within a generation, the incident had been absorbed into the background noise of frontier hardship.

---

**What the Vethari are:**

The Vethari are not a military force. They are not an empire. They are not invaders in any sense that implies territorial ambition or strategic planning. They are a species in a state of continuous neurological crisis, operating a ship that their ancestors built during a period of coherence that ended before human civilization began.

The ship navigates autonomously. The Vethari did not design it to navigate autonomously — they designed it to be piloted by a crew of three hundred operating in the shared-perception mode that the fungus originally facilitated. The shared perception was, before the corruption, a tool: a navigation interface that allowed three hundred minds to process stellar cartography simultaneously. The ship's control systems are calibrated for three hundred inputs arriving in concert. The fungus provided the concert. When the fungus corrupted the perception into psychosis, the navigation systems continued to receive three hundred inputs. The inputs were no longer coherent. The ship's autonomous routines — designed as emergency backups — took over. The backups follow the last coherent flight plan, which was a survey route plotted before the corruption. The route visits systems. The ship arrives. The Vethari emerge. The backups do not have a protocol for what happens when the crew exits the ship and kills everything on the nearest planet. The backups were not designed for that scenario. Nobody was.

The ship has been following the same survey route for an estimated twelve hundred years. It has visited an unknown number of systems. The route is not random — it follows a pattern that correlates with stellar density and atmospheric composition, consistent with a survey mission looking for habitable worlds. The Vethari were, before the corruption, explorers. They were looking for worlds that could support life. They found them. They are still finding them. What happens when they find them is no longer what was intended.

The survey route has a return leg. The navigational data includes a final waypoint: the system the Vethari launched from. The ship is, in some residual programmatic sense, trying to go home. It has been trying to go home for twelve hundred years. The stops along the way — the worlds it visits, the populations it destroys — are waypoints on a journey that the ship's autonomous systems still classify as a survey mission. The mission has an endpoint. The ship will eventually reach it. The Vethari home system is either still there or it isn't. The ship doesn't know. The ship's systems don't have a protocol for what to do when the survey mission ends because the survey mission was designed to end with a crew of three hundred coherent minds making a decision. The crew cannot make decisions. The ship cannot stop. The route continues.

---

**The fungus:**

The organism has no name in any human taxonomy because no human taxonomist has published a paper on it. The classified research that exists — and Wren doesn't know it exists — refers to it by a case file designation: BIO-44C-7, filed under the same REF 44-C code that governs everything else.

Physical characteristics: filamentary, whitish-reddish coloration, fine enough to be mistaken for vascular tissue under casual observation. It colonizes the Vethari respiratory system and establishes connections with the central nervous system through a mechanism that human neuroscience does not have a model for. Growth rate is slow — full colonization of a single Vethari takes approximately one standard year. The connection between colonized individuals is electromagnetic, operating on frequencies that human equipment can detect but not decode. Range is limited — the shared field operates within the ship and in a radius of approximately two kilometers from any colonized individual. Outside this range, individual Vethari experience partial neurological isolation that manifests as disorientation, then grief, then catatonia.

The fungus is not sentient. It does not have goals. It is an organism that evolved to colonize a neural architecture and create connections between instances of that architecture. In its original ecological context — whatever world the Vethari came from — it may have been symbiotic. The shared perception may have been advantageous. The corruption may be the result of overgrowth — a symbiote that exceeded its parameters when the host population exceeded the density the organism was calibrated for. Three hundred minds in a ship designed for three hundred. Then the population grew. Then the fungus grew with it. Then the signal became noise. Then the noise became everything.

The fungus is transmissible to humans. This is in the classified file. The colonization rate is much slower — decades rather than a year. The neurological effects are minimal — headaches, sleep disruption, vivid dreams. No shared perception. The human neural architecture is too different for the filaments to bridge. But the biological material is there. In the fragments. In the dried traces on the hull plating. Inert, in the samples that have been filed and moved and stored. Probably inert. The word "probably" appears in the classified file seventeen times.

---

**What this means for the game:**

The Vethari never appear on screen. They are Karla. They are the thing in the next room that you can hear through the wall.

The evidence of their existence appears as:
- Fragments of hull material in Concord evidence storage (the artifact)
- REF 44-C case file references in administrative databases
- The Vael addendum, page 9 (quarantine protocol for non-standard biological contamination)
- Spacer stories that nobody believes
- The Ascendant Choir's relics, which may or may not be Vethari in origin (the Choir doesn't know; the Choir's doctrine predates the knowledge)
- The coordinates in the Kurtz figure's ledger
- Wren's memory, which he doesn't share

The Vethari are not a quest. There is no mission to fight them, find them, or stop them. They are the context that makes everything else heavier. Every contract the player runs, every system they navigate, every piece of graffiti they read — all of it happens in a galaxy where something is out there, following a twelve-hundred-year-old survey route, and the administrative response to this fact has been to file it under the same code that handles customs fraud.

---

**The Vethari and the Silt:**

There is no connection. The Vethari did not cause the Silt economy. The recycler theft was a human crime for human profit. The atmospheric collapse of the Pit was manufactured by the same system that suppresses the Vethari evidence — not because the two are related, but because the system suppresses everything inconvenient with the same tools.

This parallel is the point. The Vethari fungus corrupts a species-wide neural architecture. ATMO DEBT corrupts a system-wide economic architecture. Both produce violence without intent. Both are managed by ledgers. Both are recorded by systems that were never designed to stop them.

The Vethari are what happens when a biological system optimized for connection exceeds its parameters. The Pit is what happens when an economic system optimized for allocation exceeds its parameters. Nobody designed either outcome. Both are correct, by the grammar of the system that produced them.

---

**The moment that matters:**

A Vethari in a grief cycle, standing at a hull access point, looking at a twelve-year-old human child who has spent nine weeks learning the rhythm of the ship's neurological weather. The Vethari is conscious. The Vethari knows what it is. The Vethari knows that in three seconds the grief will pass and the shared field will clear and every mind on the ship will see what the Vethari sees.

The Vethari breaks its own neck with its hands.

The motion is precise. Not violent — precise. The old ledger-system, applied one last time. The debt of having seen the child is balanced by ensuring the child is not seen by others. The entry is closed. The method of closure is the only one available.

The player never sees this moment. Wren never describes it.

THEY KNEW THE MASS. The Vethari knew it first.
