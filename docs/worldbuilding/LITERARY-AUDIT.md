# SPACEFACE — LITERARY AUDIT

*A synthesis of eight readings — le Carré, McCarthy, Ritchie, Leonard, Bacigalupi, plus a corn auditor, a humor auditor, and a prose surgeon — each given the whole corpus and told to find the greatness and the failure at the level of craft. The raw reports live in `_critiques/`. This is the verdict.*

---

## The finding that matters: there are two writers in this bible

I sent eight readers in wanting different, incompatible things. Ritchie wanted the floor to drop out from under a laugh. McCarthy wanted no laugh at all. Bacigalupi wanted a balance sheet; Leonard wanted a man to stop talking. They were chosen to fight.

They came back agreeing — which is the only result that can't be argued with.

> **McCarthy:** "two corpora wearing one cover... that corpus cannot stop telling you what the first corpus means."
> **Leonard:** "There are two voices in these files... Fire the second one. It's been explaining the first one's work to death."
> **Humor:** "a writer... who knows that the cleanest joke and the worst confession are the same sentence, and a second writer who keeps elbowing in to make sure you noticed the corpses."
> **Wince:** "you can date his arrival: somewhere between vibe-CANONICAL and vibe-04, he stopped name-dropping McCarthy and started doing the work."
> **Prose:** "a reader can watch the costume go on and come off within a single file."

The first writer **shows**. He writes *"It was designed for one adult technician. It fit two children if neither of them moved"* and lets a planet end overhead without raising his voice. The second writer **tells**. He writes *"the weight is the point"* and *"It might weigh everything,"* and he name-drops the authors he's hoping you'll mistake him for.

Everything below is a map of where the second writer needs to be fired. The good news — and it is the most hopeful thing in this document — is that he doesn't need to be *out-written*. He needs to be *deleted*. As the prose surgeon put it: cut the self-commentary and the bible loses forty percent of its word count and none of its meaning.

---

## I. Protect this — the canon the next pass must not touch

This is half the assignment and the more important half, because the failure mode you've told me you fear — converging to safe — kills exactly these lines first. They are distinctive, and distinctive is fragile. Mark them. A careless revision "improves" them by adding a word, and the word is the wound.

Each of these was praised *mechanistically* by at least one critic — not "this is evocative," but here is the gear that turns:

- **The boring artifact.** *"The artifact is boring. This is the point."* (PROTAGONIST.md:191) — the genre wants the relic to glow; the writer amputates the payoff and displaces all meaning onto the paperwork (*"The physical object is insignificant. The chain of custody is the intelligence,"* :201). That's the le Carré microdot: the object is nothing, the custody chain is the intelligence. Restraint used as a weapon.
- **Hale.** *"The priest does not look in the hold. The priest administers the sacrament. The hold is someone else's department."* (NPCs-CANONICAL.md:21) — the only figure praised by **all seven**. He is built entirely out of what he declines to do. A man whose seventeen-year unbroken signature streak *is* the laundering mechanism, and who is innocent by every standard he was handed. The villainy routes through the honest man, which is the only villainy you can't arrest. He alone justifies the project.
- **"It fit two children if neither of them moved."** (PROTAGONIST.md:47) — the whole catastrophe folded into a maintenance spec. No adverb, no grief, no author. The game's own thesis — the gap between the documentation and the truth — executed in one sentence, with the feeling left entirely in the reader. Leonard's best-line pick. Measure everything against it.
- **The Vethari suicide as arithmetic.** (PROTAGONIST.md:63 / VETHARI.md:88) — *"The only way to sever the connection was to die."* Three sentences, each the necessary premise of the next, marching to *was to die*. The horror reads like a proof. **This is the one place the "Only the X. Only the Y." cadence is load-bearing** — it is literally a chain of necessity, not a flourish. A revision that "varies the sentence length" here destroys the single instance where the tic is earned.
- **"Everything tastes like the apology that wasn't made."** (vibe-04-the-pit.md:11) — best-line pick of *two* critics. A class system delivered on the tongue, anchored to a chemical fact, explaining nothing. The Pit as a flavor, not a lecture.
- **The eight tells.** Kessler's thumb (*"Thumb twice on the seal. Manifest logged before scan clears,"* NPCs-CANONICAL.md:5); Quinn counting the stack once under bar light, once under UV. Character by gesture. You watch a man cheat in one clause and you're done.
- **The clean contracts.** (contracts/CANONICAL.md:95–115) — building deliberate *nothing* into the table so the horror has a ground to stand against. *"Cargo arrived."* as a punchline for a job where nothing happened. Horror needs a control group; almost nobody has the discipline to write the boring doors.
- **The reactor joke.** *"The previous owner defined 'fine' as 'hasn't killed anyone yet,' which is also how I define most of my relationships."* (PROTAGONIST.md:171) — one hinge word, *fine*, redefined for the machine and allowed to swing onto the man, who's too busy to notice it cut him. The joke and the confession are the *identical words*. The humor auditor's best line, and the proof the game can be funny without a wink.
- **The Callum reunion.** (STORY-STRUCTURE.md:50–59) — two people who never answer the question asked; they fight about *where* the question can be asked. Information changes hands while they argue about the venue. The betrayer is prosperous, polite, mildly inconvenienced. That's the version that hurts.
- **"paranoia is free and evidence costs something."** (PROTAGONIST.md:135) — a character's entire epistemology in seven words that scan and don't preen.
- **The kill feed that only counts to one** (SYMBOLISM-MOTIFS.md:145) and **the graffiti-as-leaked-database** (SYMBOLISM-MOTIFS.md:29) — the system has no aggregate for what it does, only a present-tense +1; and the wall can know your future because *the bureaucracy already filed it.* The future is administrative, not prophetic. Pure Bacigalupi.

If the next pass protects nothing else, protect these.

## II. The structural failures — these cost real design, not just deletion

Ranked by depth. These are not prose problems; they are load-bearing cracks, and three of them are in the exact places these authors say you are forbidden to flinch.

**1. The central arithmetic doesn't close — in a game *about* weight.** (Bacigalupi)
The whole moral architecture rests on a 12.4-tonne discrepancy, and the discrepancy contradicts itself across files. Drift logs a *0.4t* loss as "shipping loss" and the exchange *"doesn't audit losses under a ton"* (ATMOSPHERIC-ECONOMY.md:99) — a beautiful, true mechanism: the skim hides *below the audit threshold*. But then Kessler logs the entire *12.4-tonne* catalyst grid as *"moisture loss"* in a single cycle (NPC-ECOLOGY.md:51). Twelve-point-four tonnes of moisture, on one manifest, in a sector dying of dry air. That isn't under the threshold — it *is* the audit. You cannot have the elegance of the invisible skim and the drama of the giant theft filed under the same euphemism. **Fix:** the grid leaves on a *legal* authorization — VALE-ALA-47A, clean manifest, real code — because the point is that Vale's signature makes it lawful. "Moisture loss" is reserved for the ongoing sub-tonne skim Kessler and Drift run on the side. Then the crime that needs hiding is the *small* one; the big one was never illegal. That is more frightening, and your numbers finally reconcile.

**2. The collapse is a heist wearing the mask of an economy.** (Bacigalupi)
My tradition's deepest demand: the collapse must be a *business model* that prints every quarter, with a name in the beneficiary column. But trace the cash and the whole apparatus rests on *one event in year 3* — one grid, removed once, routed to Helios once (ATMOSPHERIC-ECONOMY.md:111). That's a heist. A heist has a beneficiary for one transaction; an economy has one *every cycle, forever.* Who books the gain in year 9? The reversion-to-Concord (ENDGAME-B7-REDESIGN.md:143) is a *non-profit* outcome as written — the state absorbing a dead sector is a liability. **The private buyer exists in a different document, unwired:** MTS *"buy the mining claims for pennies, seal the old shafts, and move the salvaged recycler grids"* (ATMOSPHERIC-ECONOMY.md:60). **Fix:** wire it. Give one inner-sector faction a *short position on the Pit* — they hold the ATMO TOKENS whose value *rises* as the Pit suffocates. Now the neglect isn't passive; someone is *defending* it, because it's an asset appreciating on their books, and they have a quarterly incentive to make sure the recyclers are never fixed. The man who profits when you can't breathe is the same man who sells you the breathing. *That's* the windup-girl move, and you have every piece of it lying unconnected.

**3. Vale is the weather and the hand on the lever — pick one.** (Bacigalupi, le Carré)
The thesis is that Vale is an *emergent function of the architecture* — *"Vale's power is that Vale determines which log is the official one."* Pure structure, genuinely terrifying. But then the corpus gives him personal fingerprints on the murder weapon: *"When Vale approved the decommissioning of Shaft 7... He signed the order"* (ATMOSPHERIC-ECONOMY.md:111). You can't have him be the weather *and* the man who chose this specific colony. And note the tell: the bible says *"Vale is not a villain. This is important"* (ANTAGONIST:15), then says it again at :43, again at :49 — and **the over-insistence is the costume.** You only have to say it once if it's true. **Fix:** take his hands off the lever. The allocation algorithm flags the asset as underutilized and *recommends* reallocation; Vale signs the recommendation the way Hale initials the second fine — because it's procedurally correct and refusing would cost effort he has no reason to spend. Then Vale *is* Hale, one floor up, and "not a villain" never needs saying because it's structurally true.

**4. A species of accountants whose apocalypse has no creditor.** (Bacigalupi)
You built the Vethari as *"accountants of violence, precise and transactional"* (PROTAGONIST.md:27) — a civilization that was a ledger — and then gave their apocalypse no entry in the beneficiary column. *"Nobody designed either outcome"* (VETHARI.md:37). Beautifully written, and in the one register this whole bible exists to refuse: cosmic horror, disaster with no line item. The Vethari plague can stay authorless — but the **suppression** of it cannot. *"This is not a cover-up... This is bureaucratic inertia"* (VETHARI.md:15) — and inertia is *free*, which is the one thing nothing in this world is. Someone is spending budget to keep that sealed evidence moving for fourteen years. **Fix:** find the man whose division exists *because* the secret exists — whose budget would evaporate if "probably inert" (seventeen probablys, VETHARI.md:53) became "not inert." The fragment isn't just proof of aliens; it's an uninsured hazardous-materials shipment moving through civilian logistics — through the player's own hold — because acknowledging the risk costs more than the risk. Put a P&L behind the silence and the cosmic horror becomes an *economic* one, which is the only kind that earns its place here.

**5. Eight NPCs, one story. An orchestra, not an ensemble.** (Ritchie, le Carré)
This is the failure I sent Ritchie to hunt and it's here in the open. Kessler shaves weight → feeds the theft. Mira swaps the manifest → feeds the theft. Slate's bad weld → feeds the theft. Eight instruments playing one chord. But the engine you've *almost* built is the opposite — *"They are not colleagues. They are rhymes"* (NPC-ECOLOGY.md:60), eight petty crooks whose separate larcenies sum, with no one's intent, to the murder of a planet's air. That's the real machine, and it's worth the whole project. What's missing is the **strut**: each of the eight needs a private, self-important caper *they* think is the real story. Quinn isn't skimming change — Quinn is convinced he's going to buy the bar, then the station, then his way off the Pit's hierarchy forever; he thinks the *player* is a mark in *his* long game. He's wrong. He's a node. And his not-knowing is exactly where the laugh and the pity both live. Le Carré's version of the same note: *does every character believe he's doing right?* Right now the eight are functions, not believers. Give them interiority and they become people who think they're protagonists and are wrong — which is Ritchie and le Carré asking for the same thing in two accents.

**6. Every scheme works — so there is no irony to mine.** (Ritchie)
Vale's system works. The eight-node theft executes start to finish. Callum's betrayal goes clean. The closed-loop custody closes. *Everything works* — and a world where every plan succeeds has no gap between intention and result, which is where all irony lives. **Fix:** make the theft *too* successful. The grid arrives at Helios and it's the wrong grid — or the right grid, but Helios already had a better one, so it sits uninstalled in a warehouse for fourteen years, a 12.4-tonne paperweight, while the Pit suffocates for an object nobody at the top ever needed. Five men killed the air over a thing a depot manager three rungs up had shelved by Thursday. And the punchline Ritchie hands you for free: somewhere at Helios, a functionary filed a maintenance ticket because the new lounge air *"smelled faintly of machine oil for a week"* during the install. Give me that ticket number. Forty thousand people went gray, and the only record at the destination is a *smell complaint.* The horror is twice as heavy wearing a clown's nose.

**7. The endgame has two contradictory endings stapled together.** (Ritchie)
Choice C says *"The jump didn't kill them. The wormhole didn't open"* (ENDGAME:61) — but the design note (ENDGAME:65) and the spine (STORY-SPINE:141, *"the last received message before the reactor fails"*) describe a *reactor death.* Does the player die or loop? Pick one. Ritchie's vote, and mine: the loop. *Death is an exit, and the system doesn't grant exits.* That's the only joke in the building that is also the thesis.

## III. The prose disease — these are amputations, pure deletion

Where Section II costs design decisions, this section costs nothing but nerve. Every item here is removed, not rewritten.

**1. The motif clubbed to death.** Weight / mass / ledger / debt is a real spine, hugged until it goes limp. The worst single instance — nominated worst-line by *two* critics — is **STORY-STRUCTURE.md:79: *"It weighs 0.01 tonnes. It might weigh everything."*** The first sentence is true and cold; the second is the narrator leaning into frame to make sure you felt it, converting a hard number into a soft abstraction in the space of a period. *End on the number.* The universe doesn't owe Wren that the file weighs everything; it weighs 0.01 tonnes, and the reader will decide it's a great deal — *themselves*, which is the only way it counts.
And it's **verbatim self-plagiarism**, provably: *"The Pit is everywhere. It just has different paint"* appears word-for-word in two files (SECTOR-GRADIENT.md:77, vibe-04:39); *"The count never ends. Only the counters change"* in two more. One skeleton — *[X] is always the same. Only the [Y] changes* — wearing five costumes (PROTAGONIST.md:215, SYMBOLISM:15, factions:59...). A motif is supposed to mean *more* the tenth time; verbatim recurrence means *less*, because the reader learns the shape and stops reading the words. **Fix:** one canonical home per couplet; everywhere else, write the *specific* version — what is the Pit, in *this* sector, actually made of.

**2. Author name-dropping as a substitute for the achievement.** *"This is the Le Carré insight"* (ANTAGONIST:49); *"the McCarthy version of this ending"* (ENDGAME:65); *"the Conrad answer"* (ENDGAME:127); the whole Smiley exegesis (ENDGAME:119–125). When Hale's passage *enacts* Greene without naming him, it works. When a doc says "this is the Le Carré insight" and then states the insight, the naming has replaced the achieving. If the ending is Smiley, I'll feel Smiley; if you have to write his name in the margin, it isn't. **Fix:** strike every dead author's surname from the body prose. Keep them only in a clearly fenced craft appendix, marked as scaffolding, not voice. (This audit is allowed to name them. The game is not.)

**3. The "this is the point" / "this is not X, it is Y" reflex.** Worn smooth as a nervous habit: *"The artifact is boring. This is the point"*; *"What they do to planets is not conquest"*; *"Vale is not a villain"* (×3); *"Hale is not corrupt. This is the point."* When the same scaffolding holds up every paragraph, the reader stops hearing argument and starts hearing tic. **Fix:** ration to once per document. By the third "this is the point," the only point is that the writer doesn't trust you.

**4. The writers'-room talking with the door open.** The *"agy note"* parentheticals left inside the canon (ENDGAME:65, :93, :113); the headings that announce a thesis (*"DOES THE STORY EARN ITS VIOLENCE?"*, STORY-STRUCTURE:115); the reveals that re-litigate their own subtlety (*"the mechanism is intentionally never stated,"* then three numbered explanations). A world that keeps announcing its restraint doesn't have restraint; it has anxiety about whether you noticed. **Fix:** move every line of self-commentary into a fenced `DESIGN RATIONALE` block at the foot of each file. The reveal and the defense of the reveal cannot share a paragraph.

**5. The graffiti that breaks its own law.** The architecture rests on graffiti being *testimony* — fact, name, number, cut off. Mostly it holds (*"KESSLER. SCALE 4. TYCHO. TWENTY-TWO MONTHS. LOOK IT UP."*). Then it reaches for a maxim — *"Clean means no witnesses left"* (contracts:16), *"We made it empty"* (contracts:34) — and becomes the author in a mask. *"We made it empty"* is a writer's chiasmus, too pleased with itself to be a thing a dying hand scratches in a hold. **Fix:** when the wall reaches for theme, convert it back to fact. Let the manifest line contradict the word "clean" and let the reader supply the rest.

## IV. The one honest disagreement — and how it resolves

I won't paper over the one place the panel split, because the split is useful.

**Ritchie** says you drained the comedy: *"you've kept the diamond and thrown away the dog,"* there isn't one laugh in twenty files, put the strut back. **McCarthy** says the opposite — the solemnity is the achievement; the failure is the editorial *commentary*, not the absence of jokes; a joke at the wrong moment would be the author flinching.

They're both right, and the humor auditor supplies the blade that cuts the knot: **does the silence cost the *character* something, or the *author* something?**

> The Vethari neck-break pays — the absence of wit *is* the craft. The crawlspace pays. The prison-ship math pays. Keep them airless; a joke there is the author losing his nerve.
> The atmospheric-economy body prose *doesn't* pay — it's not restrained, it's just careful. A Pit worker who never once gets to be funny about suffocating isn't stoic; he's unwritten.

So the resolution is not "add jokes" (that wrecks the McCarthy material) and not "stay solemn" (that's why the economy docs read as a spreadsheet). It's surgical: **the missing strut and the missing wit belong to the eight NPCs and the Pit's shop floor — the lived, economic, Ritchie register. The Vethari, the crawlspace, the silo stay silent.** The game already invented the shop-floor wit it needs — *"Vale's Breath," "breathing gray"* — and then rationed itself to none of it in the body text. Spend some.

## V. Verdict

It earns its existence — and I write that rarely, because the bar these five authors set is not popularity, it's whether a sentence can stand in their company without flinching. A dozen of these can. The intelligence is real and *unfakeable in the specific way*: characterization done by grammar (the card table is a simpler system than a genocide), horror delivered by withholding (the boring artifact), a villain you cannot arrest (Hale), an apocalypse enacted as a proof (the neck-break). When this writer is being le Carré, he is the genuine article.

The failures are equally specific, and they are *habits*, not deficits — which is the most damning and the most hopeful thing I can say. The costume shows in three structural places where the tradition forbids flinching: the central weight-arithmetic contradicts itself; the collapse is a one-time heist with no recurring beneficiary; and the species of accountants gets an apocalypse with no creditor. And it shows in one prose addiction worn through every file — the motif recited where it should be felt, the dead authors invoked where they should be embodied, the self-commentary that grades its own homework inside the canon. The bible's own best instruction is the rule it breaks: *if every door opens onto a crime, the reveals stop working* — and right now every door opens onto the mass.

The strangest verdict is the truest one, and the humor auditor landed it first: **right now the bible is funnier, and colder, and better, than the game it describes — because its sharpest writing is stranded in italic design-notes the player will never read.** Promote the marginalia into the artifact. Fire the second writer. Let the manifest carry the weight it already carries. Then the costume comes off, and what's underneath has been the real thing the whole time.

## VI. What I'd do next — in order, sorted by what it costs

**Amputations (no decisions required; safe to start now on the master-branch docs):**

2. Strip every dead-author surname from body prose; relocate to a fenced craft appendix.
3. Dedupe the verbatim couplets — one home each, specific version everywhere else.
4. Ration the "this is the point" / "not X, it is Y" tic to once per document.
5. Move all `agy note` / self-commentary into `DESIGN RATIONALE` blocks; delete the thesis headings.
6. Retire vibe-CANONICAL's Borderlands/Firefly register in favor of the colder voice the rest of the bible has already grown into. *(You are mid-molt on this one already.)*

**Structure (these cost design calls — yours to make):**
7. Close the arithmetic: legal authorization for the 12.4t grid; "moisture loss" reserved for the sub-tonne skim.
8. Wire the recurring beneficiary: give MTS a short position on the Pit's air.
9. Take Vale's hands off the lever — algorithm recommends, Vale initials.
10. Put a budget behind the Vethari silence.
11. Give the eight NPCs their struts; make one scheme backfire upward into a windfall nobody planned.
12. Resolve the endgame contradiction (vote: the loop).

**The blocker that gates the structural work:** fourteen of these files exist only on the `codex/quality-preserving-performance` branch, not in the working tree. Every Section-II fix and most of Section III touches them. That's a branch-reconciliation call I won't make silently — it has blast radius and it's yours.
