Based on the worldbuilding documents in the `docs/worldbuilding/` directory, here is how the narrative resolves these Conradian elements:

### 1. The Missing Conrad Element: Self-Recognition
In Joseph Conrad’s *Heart of Darkness*, the core horror is not Kurtz’s external savagery, but Marlow's recognition of Kurtz as his own reflection. Marlow realizes that the "civilized" European ivory trade and Kurtz's unmasked brutality are part of the exact same exploitative machine. 

In *SpaceFace*, the missing element is the player’s realization that their journey from the core to the frontier is not an escape from corruption, but a journey inward to confront the mechanism that already marked them. They are not independent contractors operating outside the system; they are the physical enforcement of it.

### 2. The Moment of Recognition
The player's recognition of themselves in the Administrator occurs in two ways:
*   **The Ledger Entry:** In the Administrator’s handwritten journal ([ENDGAME-B7-REDESIGN.md](file:///C:/Users/93rob/Documents/GitHub/SpaceFace/docs/worldbuilding/story/ENDGAME-B7-REDESIGN.md#L109-L115)), the player finds their own callsign in the `COUNTERPARTY` column. Crucially, the entry is dated **six weeks before the player's first contract**, filed under the ship’s transponder ID. The player was a pre-allocated asset in Vale's system before they even stepped into the cockpit.
*   **The Atmospheric Resonance:** When the player reaches the Administrator’s derelict station at Ashfall Reach ([SECTOR-GRADIENT.md](file:///C:/Users/93rob/Documents/GitHub/SpaceFace/docs/worldbuilding/story/SECTOR-GRADIENT.md#L57-L66)), they find that the station runs at **14 degrees Celsius** and smells of stale hydraulic fluid and organic matter. This is the exact temperature and smell of the lower decks of **The Pit (S0)** where the player started. The furthest edge of lawlessness is structurally identical to home.
