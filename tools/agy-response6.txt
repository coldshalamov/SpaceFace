### 1. Critique of the Existing Four Choices

#### **Choice A: THE CLEAN UNIFORM**
*   **Tone Match:** **Perfect (Le Carré).**
*   **Critique:** This is highly effective. It mirrors George Smiley’s realization that the "good guys" use the exact same methods, ledgers, and lies as their enemies. The player escapes their criminal record only to become the administrative hand that starves their home sector. 
*   **Verdict:** Keep as is. It captures the essence of survival at the cost of dignity and self-respect.

#### **Choice B: THE SAME SILENCE**
*   **Tone Match:** **Medium (Slightly too neat).**
*   **Critique:** In its current form, this feels a bit like a shadow-mastermind power fantasy. In a Le Carré or McCarthy universe, hiding in the routing tables doesn't grant comfort; it grants absolute, paralyzing paranoia. The coordinator who profits from every war is also the first target when a route fails.
*   **Adjustment to earn the tone:** De-emphasize the "better insulation" and highlight the isolation. The player shouldn't feel like a smug logistics boss; they should feel like an algorithm running in a dark, windowless room, terrified of every unexpected comms ping. They profit, but they can never spend it because their identity has been erased. The system didn't liberate them—it just turned them into a pipe.
